Gimbap

Description

Kimbap is a Korean rice roll filled with various ingredients like vegetables, pickled radish, and often meat or seafood, rolled in seaweed sheets.

Simply spread seasoned rice on a seaweed sheet, add your desired fillings, and roll tightly before slicing into bite-sized pieces.

Ingredients

  • seaweed sheets
  • rice
  • vegetables
  • pickled radish

Steps

  1. Cook short-grain rice and season it with a mixture of rice vinegar, sugar, and salt.
  2. Place a bamboo sushi rolling mat on a flat surface, then put a sheet of roasted seaweed (gim) on top.
  3. Spread a thin layer of seasoned rice evenly over the seaweed, leaving a small margin at the top.
  4. Arrange your choice of fillings, such as julienned vegetables, pickled radish, cooked and seasoned proteins (like beef, crab, or egg strips), along the center of the rice.
  5. Roll the seaweed and rice over the fillings using the bamboo mat, applying gentle pressure to create a tight cylinder.
  6. Moisten the edge of the seaweed with a bit of water to seal the roll.
  7. Using a sharp knife, slice the roll into bite-sized pieces.
  8. Repeat the process with the remaining ingredients.
  9. Serve the kimbap slices with soy sauce or a dipping sauce of your choice.
